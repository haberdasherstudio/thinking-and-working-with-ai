Is this for you?

This workshop is for people who sense AI is going to shape the rest of their lives and careers, but don’t want to outsource their mind, their taste, or their humanity in the process.

  • You’re a creative (writer, musician, artist, performer) who wants AI to amplify your work, not flatten it.
  • You’re a professional who’s tired of hearing about AI and wants to actually integrate it into your workflow.
  • You’re AI-curious but overwhelmed by the noise, and want a grounded, human guide.
  • You’d like to build projects (content, courses, books, apps, offers) and use AI without losing your own voice.
No prior technical background required Beginner–intermediate AI users welcome You, a laptop, and a willingness to experiment

What you’ll learn

Each week combines live teaching, live demos, and time for questions. You’ll see real workflows in action and build your own as we go.

Week 1 · Foundations

Thinking with AI

Build the core mental models for working with AI: breaking down ideas, structuring prompts, iterating with intention, and avoiding common traps like vague asks and over-trusting the output. We’ll ground this in clarity, curiosity, and conscience instead of hype.

Week 2 · Practice

AI in Action: Writing, Images, Audio & Research

See AI used across real creative and practical tasks: outlining and drafting writing, exploring visual concepts, supporting music and audio workflows, and doing serious research. You’ll build a small project of your own using the tools you already have access to.

Week 3 · Systems

Building Your Personal AI Workflow

Design a repeatable system for your life and work: how AI fits into your week, how to use it without getting scattered, and how to turn it into a creative and strategic partner rather than a distraction.

Week 4 · Perspective

Staying Human in an Accelerating AI World

Explore what it means to keep your voice, your judgment, and your sense of meaning intact as AI accelerates. We’ll talk about identity, creativity, agency, and how to keep using these tools in a way that feels aligned with the kind of life you actually want to live.

About your instructor

Derek Bernard

Derek is a writer, musician, educator, and performer based in San Francisco. He’s published books, released original music, taught guitar and voice for years, and explored AI as a creative and practical collaborator across writing, music, app-building, and visual storytelling.

His approach is part rigor, part play: philosophical enough to take the implications of AI seriously, and grounded enough to keep things human, useful, and usable in everyday life.

This workshop is a distillation of what’s actually worked for him in his own life, plus what he’s seen work with students and fellow creatives navigating this new terrain.
